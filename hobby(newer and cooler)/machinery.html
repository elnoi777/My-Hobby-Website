Fun Fact

Hydropower is one of the oldest energy sources, used for mechanical power (like grinding grain with waterwheels) for millennia (ancient Greece/China) long before electricity, but the first hydroelectric plant for electricity was in 1878 at Cragside, England, followed by Appleton, Wisconsin in 1882.

Fun Fact

The earliest known concept for an automatic door with a sensor system was developed in the 1st century AD by Heron of Alexandria, a Greek mathematician and inventor. His system ingeniously used steam power and a series of pulleys to open the door, rather than modern electricity or infrared beams.
